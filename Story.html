THE BATTLE OF THE THRONE

"The tales are old and the lies are told, Witness a new line in a story time"

Once upon a time, there lived two great snakes from rival kingdoms. In one kingdom ruled the King Snake, the absolute sovereign of all snakes. His authority stretched across every land, and even those of royal blood bowed before him.

In the other kingdom lived the Prince Snake, second in command and ruler over half of the snake realms. The Prince Snake hungered for more. He despised living under the shadow of another ruler—especially one who claimed dominion over him. Power was divided, but his ambition was not. He desired one kingdom, one crown, and one ruler. To claim absolute control, he issued a challenge: a death battle known as the Battle of the Throne.

The battle shook the earth. Fangs clashed, scales tore, and venom spilled upon the ground. Both warriors bore deep cuts across their bodies, their scars marking each strike exchanged.

At last, with strength nearly spent, the Prince Snake launched his final attack. With a sudden lunge, he drove his fangs into the King Snake’s eye. Venom surged into the brain, and death came instantly.

The Prince Snake stood victorious. No longer a prince, he rose as the sole ruler of all serpents. Thus, he became the King of All Snakes.

By: Alphonsus O.
